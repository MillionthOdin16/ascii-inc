# Artist Onboarding Checklist
*Ascii Inc | Version 1.0 | 2026-04-01*

---

## Why This Checklist

A smooth onboarding experience is the difference between an artist who uploads three pieces and sells within a week versus one who never comes back. This checklist walks new artists through every step from signup to first sale.

**Goal:** Get artists to their first upload and first sale as quickly as possible. Every step should feel achievable in under 10 minutes.

---

## Phase 1: Account Setup (Est. 10 minutes)

### 1.1 Sign Up
- [ ] Visit [asciinc.com/artists](https://asciinc.com/artists) and click "Apply to Sell"
- [ ] Sign up with email address (Google OAuth recommended for fastest onboarding)
- [ ] Verify email address (check inbox, click verification link within 15 minutes)
- [ ] Set a display name (this appears on your public profile -- use your artist name or pseudonym)

### 1.2 Profile Basics
- [ ] Upload a profile avatar (square image, minimum 200x200px; ASCII art made in the platform is ideal)
- [ ] Write a bio (aim for 2-4 sentences: who you are, what kind of ASCII art you make, what inspires you)
- [ ] Add your location (city/country -- optional, but collectors appreciate knowing)
- [ ] Add social links (Twitter/X, Instagram, personal website, DeviantArt, etc.)

### 1.3 Payment Setup
- [ ] Navigate to Account Settings > Payouts
- [ ] Connect a Stripe account (required for receiving payments)
  - If no Stripe account: click "Create Stripe account" and follow prompts (takes ~5 minutes)
  - Stripe handles all payment processing; Ascii Inc never touches your banking info
- [ ] Verify identity with Stripe (required for payouts over $1,000/year -- happens automatically when you cross the threshold)
- [ ] Set your payout schedule (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly -- default is monthly)
- [ ] Add a minimum payout threshold if desired (default: $10)

## Phase 2: First Listing (Est. 15-20 minutes)

### 2.1 Upload Your Artwork
- [ ] Navigate to "My Listings" > "New Listing"
- [ ] Paste your ASCII art into the editor (or upload a .txt file)
  - Tip: Use a monospace font preview to see how it will render for collectors
  - Tip: Avoid line lengths over 120 characters for best cross-platform display
- [ ] Add alt text / description of the art (important for accessibility and SEO)
- [ ] Preview how the listing looks on desktop and mobile

### 2.2 Set Pricing
Pricing tiers (guidance, not enforced):
- **Small/simple** (small portraits, simple logos, single icons): $15-$30
- **Medium complexity** (character art, detailed scenes, multi-panel): $50-$100
- **Large/complex** (full scenes, detailed illustrations, large-scale pieces): $150-$300

- [ ] Set your price based on the complexity and time invested
- [ ] Check the "Compare similar listings" toggle to see what similar artists charge
- [ ] Note: Ascii Inc takes a 20% platform fee. You keep 80% of every sale.

### 2.3 Add Listing Details
- [ ] Title: descriptive but concise (e.g., "Fox Portrait in Monospace", "Terminal Sunset Scene")
- [ ] Description: 2-5 sentences describing the piece, the technique, what inspired it
- [ ] Tags: add 3-5 relevant tags (e.g., `#portrait`, `#nature`, `#retro`, `#monochrome`)
- [ ] Theme category: select from the available categories (Characters, Landscapes, Objects, Typography, Animations, etc.)
- [ ] Resolution/size: select from Small (<500 chars), Medium (500-2000), Large (2000+)

### 2.4 Publish
- [ ] Review the listing preview one final time
- [ ] Click "Publish Listing"
- [ ] Listing goes live immediately (no approval waiting period)

## Phase 3: Storefront Customization (Est. 10 minutes)

### 3.1 Personal Storefront
- [ ] Navigate to "My Storefront"
- [ ] Upload a storefront banner (recommended: 1500x500px, or wide ASCII art banner)
- [ ] Write your storefront intro text (a 1-2 paragraph welcome message for visitors)
- [ ] Arrange your listings in your preferred order (drag-and-drop)
- [ ] Enable/disable "Featured listing" on your best piece

### 3.2 Public Profile Check
- [ ] Visit your public profile URL and review how it looks to collectors
- [ ] Check that avatar, bio, links, and listings all display correctly
- [ ] Test that clicking a listing takes you to the purchase page

## Phase 4: Promotion & Getting Your First Sale

### 4.1 Share Your Listing
- [ ] Share your listing on Twitter/X (tag @asciinc)
- [ ] Share on Reddit (r/asciiart, r/DigitalArt -- be genuine, not spammy)
- [ ] Add a link in your email signature
- [ ] Tell existing followers / collectors you already have

### 4.2 Optimize for Discovery
- [ ] Use specific, searchable titles (collectors search by subject, style, mood)
- [ ] Add tags that describe the subject matter and art style
- [ ] Refresh listings periodically (editing a listing bumps its position in search)
- [ ] Post regularly -- artists with 5+ active listings get 3x more discovery impressions

### 4.3 Tips for Compelling Listings
- **Show scale:** Include a reference (e.g., "printed at 8x10 inches" or "fills a terminal window") so collectors understand the size.
- **Tell the story:** ASCII art is technical and artistic. Share what you were thinking when you made it.
- **High-contrast preview:** Make sure your ASCII art looks good in the preview thumbnail.
- **Responsive display:** Test your art at different window widths -- monospaced art can reflow unexpectedly.
- **Multiple angles:** For complex pieces, consider including a version with line numbers (for counting) or a zoomed view.

## Phase 5: Ongoing Success

### 5.1 Post-Sale
- [ ] You'll receive an email notification when someone purchases
- [ ] Delivery is automated -- the collector receives their download link immediately
- [ ] Respond to any collector messages within 24 hours
- [ ] Consider sending a thank-you note to your collector (builds loyalty)

### 5.2 Analytics
- [ ] Check "My Stats" weekly to see views, favorites, and sales by listing
- [ ] Identify which listings get the most views but fewest sales -- consider revising the description or price
- [ ] Track which channels drive the most referral traffic

### 5.3 Grow Your Presence
- [ ] Upload at least 1 new listing per week (active artists rank higher in search)
- [ ] Participate in community contests (Ascii Inc monthly contests, r/asciiart events)
- [ ] Consider a limited-edition series (numbered editions command higher prices and urgency)
- [ ] Ask satisfied collectors to leave a review

## FAQ

**Q: Can I sell the same piece on Ascii Inc and elsewhere?**
A: Yes. You retain full rights to your work. You can sell the same piece on other platforms. However, you cannot sell the same listing (same piece, same resolution) on Ascii Inc more than once -- digital files are delivered to each buyer individually.

**Q: How do I handle custom commissions?**
A: Ascii Inc does not currently support custom commission workflows. Handle commissions outside the platform (direct message, PayPal, etc.) and list the completed commission on Ascii Inc for the buyer's archival copy.

**Q: What if a buyer disputes a purchase?**
A: Ascii Inc's dispute policy covers non-delivery or corrupt files. Contact support within 7 days of purchase. We recommend always delivering your files within 24 hours of a sale.

**Q: Can I offer discounts?**
A: Yes. You can create discount codes for individual listings or your entire storefront at any time.
